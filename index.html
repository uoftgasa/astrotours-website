Discover the Universe!

On the first Thursday of most months, the free Astronomy Public Tours – the AstroTours – are held by the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Toronto.

We bring to you the hottest discoveries of modern astrophysics, the life and times of astronomers past, and the science behind blockbuster films and science fiction. Our talks are meant for a diverse audience, and we try to keep the technical aspects of them to a degree understandable by high school students.

Public Talk

A 45-minute presentation on a topic in astronomy

Telescope Viewing

See the stars through the telescopes in our observatory (weather permitting)

Interactive Media

Oculus Rift, Worldwide Telescope, and 3D-printed models

Planetarium Shows

Experience the cosmos in our 25-seat planetarium (tickets available here)

We are dedicated to accessibility and do our best to accommodate guests of all needs.

We wish to acknowledge this land on which the University of Toronto operates. For thousands of years it has been the traditional land of the Huron–Wendat, the Seneca, and most recently, the Mississaugas of the Credit River. Today, this meeting place is still the home to many Indigenous people from across Turtle Island and we are grateful to have the opportunity to work on this land.
